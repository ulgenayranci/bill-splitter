## Recurring strategic themes (across all docs)
1. **Product clarity is the #1 priority (founder, 2026-06-02).** LilySplit reads as a clearer, more focused product. Match its simplicity without losing our depth. Being addressed via flow redesign in Claude design.
2. **No-account / link-based is now table stakes, not a moat.** LilySplit ships and markets it as hard as we planned to. The wedge must be clarity + AI quality.
3. **OCR accuracy + AI cleanup is our clearest quality moat.** Competitors "itemize" raw OCR; our abbreviation expansion is differentiated — only if it reliably strips non-item lines.
4. **Differentiate on collaboration depth** — real-time claiming, shared items, quantity, per-person tips, host controls. LilySplit/Tab are comparatively flat.
5. **Non-US / currency is our lane (P6).** We are explicitly *not* chasing the US market; the field is US/Venmo-centric.
6. **The emotional job is social, not arithmetic** — removing awkwardness/quibbling at the table.
7. **Drinks/alcohol asymmetry** is the most charged fairness complaint — first-class drink handling differentiates.
8. **Invest in SEO/comparison content** — LilySplit is winning discovery via blog/comparison posts.

> **Explicitly out of scope:** Venmo / payment settlement. Not US-focused → payment-rail integration is not a goal. Disregard the "close the payment loop" framing from earlier Reddit research.
